Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers include:

John Hancock
His large, flamboyant signature is perhaps the most famous, to the point where "John Hancock" is a synonym for a signature itself. He was the President of the Continental Congress at the time and signed first.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration of Independence, he later became the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A renowned polymath, inventor, diplomat, and statesman, he was the oldest signer at 70 years old. His international fame preceded the Revolution.
John Adams
A fierce advocate for independence, he was a key figure in the Continental Congress and later served as the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A prominent radical and organizer of the American Revolution, known for his fiery rhetoric and role in inciting rebellion.
Roger Sherman
A well-rounded statesman from Connecticut, he was one of only two individuals to sign the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Roman Catholic signer, he was also one of the wealthiest.

These individuals, through their leadership, intellect, and commitment, played pivotal roles in articulating the ideals of American independence and shaping the nation's early foundations.
